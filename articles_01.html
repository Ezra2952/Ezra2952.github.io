Do's and Don'ts of Online Classes

Kyaw Nyi Nyi Khant, July 13, 2020

Due to the rapid advancement of technology students nowadays can study entirely online while still socializing with classmates, watching lectures and participating in subject-specific discussions. This method of taking courses online instead of in a physical classroom is called online learning. Online learning allows students to study and learn from the comfort of their bedrooms, on their couches, and in their living rooms.

With the effects of current COVID-19 cases, students around the world are engaging more and more in online learning to be safe from the virus and to continue their education. To be a better online student, here are some definite do’s and don’ts for students when participating in online learning. 

The Do’s of Online Classes

1

Be punctual 

Just like in physical classrooms, online classes require attendance. Therefore, you need to make your devices and yourselves ready to log into the online platform that their professor is using on time. Since the technologies the you are using for online classes are not far from you, being late is not acceptable.

2

Read course syllabus 

Before the classes start, you should read the syllabus of the respective subjects. Based on the syllabus provided by each subjects’ professors, you need to read the required readings, prepare for subject discussions and check whether there are announcements regarding to your assignments. By doing this, you can successfully pass and excel in the courses you’ve enrolled in.

3

Introduce yourself

Introduce yourself to your instructor and this will make a huge difference. Send your professor an email saying, ‘This is who I am, here’s what I’m studying, and this is why I’m in your class’. By doing this, it will make you stand you stand out and your professor will receive a positive impression on you.

4

Test your tech

Before participating in an online class, you need to make sure that there are no problems with your technology. These problems include internet, microphone, speaker, camera and battery problems. Therefore, early testing and preparation of your required technologies are necessary to prevent your online learning from having difficulties.  

5

Embrace being flexible

As an online learner, you’ll likely be familiar with the appeal of this type of learning -- setting your own schedule, working from home, and more. Being flexible is important to become a successful online learner. It’s important that you manage your time effectively, make sure you allow enough time and space in your day to log on.

The Don’ts of Online Classes

1

Don’t be a wallflower

Just like in physical classrooms, students in online classes also need to be active participants. Professors will be expecting you to participate even in online classes. Being shy and being a wallflower will only affect your education in a bad way. Therefore, you should communicate more with your professor and other students to engage in online class discussions.

2

Don’t make excuse for bad tech

As students are enrolled in an online class, the professor will expect you to be able to set up and prepare your technology accordingly to log in on time and participate fully. Additionally, if there are problems with uploading assignments you should email it to your instructor as a backup. This will prove that you’re not making any excuses that you haven’t done your assignment on time. After all, since you’re the one who decided to sign up for an online class, you should take responsibility to ensure your technology works properly.

3

Don’t get distracted to other online resources

When attending an online class, students should focus on the class and not be distracted by other online additional resources on the internet. Since internet is a place which has many features students can be easily distracted and surf internet for things that doesn’t concern with their studies. Therefore, you should always block pop-ups, turn off notifications and logged out of social media accounts when participating in an online class.

4

Don’t think you need to go through it alone 

Online learning can often be very lonely at times. To change this feeling, you should always talk to other students and make friends with them. If making friends with strangers online is awkward for you, there can be times where you professor will make it easier by assigning groups for group assignments. In this way, make friends with your group members while you have the chance. Don’t think you’re in it all alone. In fact, there’s an entire group of other students in the same boat as you.

5

Don’t be afraid to speak up

Sometimes, students are often scared of their professors. Even though, the instructors are in charge, students should keep in mind that they are paying for the course. They should expect to get a response from their instructors in a reasonable amount of time. If your instructor isn’t answering you, question that.
