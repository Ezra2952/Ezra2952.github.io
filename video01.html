Daily Lifestyle of International Student at Singapore 

by Lin Kyaw Shin

Hello everyone my name is Lin Kyaw Shin. Currently, I studying Marketing and international business major at James cook university Singapore. Today, I would like to share you guys about daily lifestyles the the international students in Singapore. First of all, I am going to explain about some detail. For me, I usually choose classes which are started around 9 am hence I get up at 8 am . After that I take a bath and prepare to go to school. For the transportation, Both bus stop and MRT station are near the uni so it is very convenient .

As for me, the bus stop is at the corner of the street so I went to school by bus. Since, there are only few traffic jam in Singapore. It only take 7 min from my apartment to Uni. For the breakfast, I normally have at school canteen because the meal are more cheaper than outside food court and more delicious. After that, I attend my first class. There are about 2 or 3 classes in a day. We have to attend around 8 classes per week including L + T. The teacher are really kind and helpful. Especially, in the T classes, unlike L classes there are only 30 Students per classes. Hence, we can easily ask the tutors which we don’t understand during L Classes. That’s mean, it is more easier to communicate with tutor.

According to the classes ,somedays the classes finished early and sometime its late. For lunch, I usually having lunch with my friends at school canteen. In our uni canteen, the Muslim shop is really famous among Myanmar student because the dishes are tastes like Myanmar cultural food. After lunch, sometime I went to the library which is well design for the student who wants to study alone quiet. For me, I usually study with my friends at the student hubs that is really perfect place for study groups. If there is not much things to study , we borrowed card board games from the student activities and play at the student hub. Every Friday, I played table tennis 3 pm to 5 pm since I join the table tennis club.


After the classes, sometime I went to bugis with my friends and have some Japanese ramens or hotpot at bugis. For dinner, I usually have at the food court near apartment. Normally, I went back around 8 pm. When I arrived I take a bath and rest on my bed or play games. I usually rest at night however if the exam or assignment due date is close. I studies at night too. Usually, I sleep at 12 pm. At the weekend, I went to peninsula plaza when I miss Myanmar cultural food. This is my daily lifestyles as the international student in Singapore. Thanks for watching and I hope you guys enjoyed this video.

For the new students, if you guys need any help you can contact us from MCC from messengers and we are warmly welcome to help you guys.

Sharing is caring!
