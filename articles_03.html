How to be effective in online classes

Thu Htoo Auant, 21st July, 2020

The COVID-19 resulted in the shutdown of schools all over the world. About 1.2 billion children worldwide are out of class. The online course has many benefits: it helps you to learn any time and everywhere that works best for you. Even so, if you're not prepared, online classes may present unique challenges. However, if you build skills to learn online on an efficient basis, you can find that the online courses can be a perfect alternative to a traditional classroom.

How to be an effective online learner!

1

Keep yourself accountable

It is important to set goals in the beginning of the semester (or trimester) and check it on a weekly basis. You often receive verbal or visual reminders of the upcoming due date in a traditional classroom setting. But without a professor actively reminding you, it is your responsibility to ensure that you have spent sufficient time to complete the assignment, so that you don't start the assignment the day before it is due.

2

Schedule time to study with efficient time management

Open your calendar and select a predictable, reliable time to watch lectures and complete assignments. Without them, you might easily to find yourself cramming before classes or handing in subpar assignments. It helps to make sure your lessons are not the last thing on your to-do list.

3

Create a dedicated study space

When you are in the same place where you learned it first, you can easily recall information. Therefore, having an area home dedicated to taking online courses will make your learning more effective. Moreover, wherever you choose, make sure there’s high-speed internet access so you’re not trying to take an online course over a lagging connection.

4

Actively participate

This is important for new students who must start the first trimester with online classes. To help you understand the course material and interact with other classmates, participate in the online chat available in the online class. Read what other students and your professor are saying, and if you have a question, ask for clarification.
And if you do feel yourself falling behind, speak up. Don’t wait until an assignment is almost due to ask questions or report issues. Email your professor and be proactive in asking for help. 

5

Eliminate distractions

You will face many distractions from Netflix to social media to chores, which can quickly interrupt your studies. The distractions vary from people to people, some might find that they can tune out a noisy home by listening to music. Others might choose to work from a local coffee shop or library to eliminate their urge to multitask at home. Ultimately, you will need to find a strategy that works best for you.

6

Motivation and Independence

A student online will want to learn in order to succeed. Online learning needs to be independent, internally motivated, accountable and mature to a certain level. Moreover, efficient learners studying online never procrastinate. They don't delay tasks or wait to submit their papers until the last moment.
Successful online students enjoy freedoms including the ability to work at their own pace and complete their work in as much time as it takes. On the other hand, they know that the best way to complete their assignments is to do it early and on time-cramming simply doesn't work, and it doesn't help you maintain long-term knowledge.
Get started on getting successful early.
