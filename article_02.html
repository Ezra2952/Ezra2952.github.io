Struggles of student life in Singapore

Ei Phyu Cin Kyaw, July 16 2020

Singapore is one of the ideal destinations in Southeast Asia for Studying Abroad for us students from Myanmar. Studying abroad, moving to a new country, and living alone, away from family is taking a whole new step out of your comfort zone and build skills and knowledge while facing several challenges. Living abroad, away from the reach of parents and experiencing the new city may seem very ideal for fresh high school graduates who are excited to explore the live outside of their comfort zone. 

Studying abroad in Singapore is really fun and exciting, but it doesn’t always work that way. There are times where students face difficulties to overcome, which will build up the skills of the students and become more independent. 

👉 Foods : Singapore have a lot of delicious foods, and have the availability of different cuisines from around the world. BUT it is really hard to find a good Burmese food, which tastes like home. Even though food here tastes superb, sometimes when we miss the taste of home it is really hard to fulfil that wish (unless you are really good at cooking Burmese food yourself😂)

👉 Transportation is really convenient in Singapore and most of the students use MRT, buses and taxis to get to school. If you live far from school, it might become a problem with the attendance and making to school on time since the public transports can get really crowded on some days.

👉 Environment : A good and comfortable environment is important is important when you are living away from home. It is important to live with a kind landlord or flatmates since the bad flatmates can give you nightmare😂. While staying away from home, it is possible to get homesick, so try to adjust your living environment feel like home. The most important part of getting over homesick and adapting a new environment is making new friends.🤝 When you enter a new environment, new school full, in a new city don't hesitate to talk to new people and make friends. Making new friends and exploring the city can help release stress and helps adapt the new environment. 

👉 Cultural shocks will be seen within the first few months coming here. Even though our countries are near, and have the similar basic understanding of culture, there are things that we need to adapt. Singapore is a country which is diverse so there will be some cultural aspects and ceremonies which students from Myanmar are not keen to. When you see someting you are not used to or unusual in your eyes, ask people around you, maybe they will be able to explain what it is and this might help you gain more knowledge and understanding of this new city you are living in. Take the easy step by trying to respect the different cultures and you will find your transition from Myanamr to Singapore smoother.

👉👉 Language : IT WOULD BE A PLUS PLUS IF YOU COULD SPEAK BASIC MANDARIN OR SPEAK FLUENTLY (if you do your life would be much easier buying food here, and the mala lady from JCU will love you😂)
